# L1.01 "WROOM breakout" — four-lens focus panel + capture shot-list

**Date:** 2026-06-11 · **Lesson:** `l1-01-wroom-breakout` @`v1` (the one fully-built OTD Academy lesson)
**Method:** read-only dump of all 8 stage cards' `contentBlocks` from the prod DB, reviewed in parallel by four personas — **Student** (true beginner), **Teacher** (instructional designer), **Engineer** (PCB/hardware SME), **Admin** (product/operator) — then synthesized. The engineer lens cross-checked concrete values and pinouts against the live parts DB (`otd-parts` MCP). Read-only: nothing in the DB or lesson was changed.

---

## Headline

- **No technical blockers.** The engineer audited every load-bearing value, pin assignment, KiCad-10 menu path, and quiz answer against datasheets and the project's own verified pinout — **all correct.** A beginner who follows this builds a working board and learns nothing wrong. The flagship lesson is technically trustworthy.
- **The real gap is *operability*, not accuracy.** The lesson teaches the *circuit* superbly but assumes the reader can already *drive KiCad*. The first KiCad gesture (place a part, drag a wire onto a pin) is never shown; the two most failure-prone procedures (LAYOUT board-setup, and the antenna keep-out) are under-illustrated or under-specified; and the three screenshots meant to carry the board-setup step **are empty placeholders right now.**
- **Highest-ROI move:** fill the **LAYOUT [10][11][12] board-setup screenshot trio** — unanimous across all four lenses, pure KiCad/browser screenshots (no physical build needed), captureHints already written, sitting on the gate stage at the exact step beginners silently fail.
- **One verified operational wart:** L1.01's BOM_SOURCING stage carries a checklist literally titled **"STRIPBOARD validation checklist" with 0 items** on a board that has nothing to do with stripboard (see [Finding BOM-1](#bom_sourcing)).
- **Two stale captions:** LAYOUT [03] and [17] are already filled with real captures but still say "*(Reference render to come.)*" — they tell the learner an image is missing when it isn't.
